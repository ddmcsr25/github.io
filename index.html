Summary

I reported security research findings affecting Google Pixel 6 (Android 14) to Google through the Android & Devices Vulnerability Reward Program (VRP/ASR). Google acknowledged the report and issued a reward. This page exists to provide a stable public reference that I was the submitting researcher.

Security Impact (High-Level)

  • Concerns the real-world risk when a device is in an ownership-verification/FRP context.
  • Highlights what an attacker with physical access could attempt under certain conditions.
  • Evaluates the behavior against a practical threat model and user-protection expectations.

Why “Intended Behavior” Can Still Matter

Some reports are classified as “intended behavior” because they fall within an accepted threat model or require conditions (e.g., physical access) that the platform treats differently from remote exploitation. Even so, documenting the risk helps users and defenders understand protections and residual gaps.

Defensive Guidance

  • Keep devices updated to the latest stable Android security patch level.
  • Use a strong device passcode and account security (2FA where possible).
  • For enterprises: consider MDM/enterprise device policies and inventory controls for devices at risk of loss/theft.
  • If you bought a used device: complete official ownership transfer steps and obtain proof of purchase.
